Frequently Asked Questions
What should I wear? ⌄
Dressy / festive is perfect. For the service, think “dressy synagogue” — suits or slacks + button-down (tie optional), and dresses or elegant separates. Comfortable shoes are a good idea for mingling and dancing.
What’s the schedule? ⌄
Please arrive 5:30 PM sharp for the service. Afterward: cocktail hour + games, then dinner, speeches, and dancing through the end of the evening.
Where do I book a hotel? ⌄
We recommend Cherry Hill / Mount Laurel / nearby Philly suburbs. If you’re booking for that weekend, reserving early helps. (We’ll share a preferred option under “Hotel Block” when available.)
How does pickup work? ⌄
Pickup is easiest at the main entrance. If you’re using a car service, set the pickup location to Congregation Kol Ami and plan to step out a few minutes before pickup time.
Are kids welcome? ⌄
If your invitation includes children, we’d love to have them — there will be games and kid-friendly fun during the celebration.
Are gifts expected? ⌄
Your presence is the best gift. If you’d like to bring something, a card is always appreciated, and a donation toward Ari’s mitzvah project is meaningful as well.
Anything else I should know? ⌄
Please silence phones during the service. Kippahs are typically available at the entrance. Most importantly: come ready to celebrate Ari!
Mitzvah Project

Ari is fundraising and volunteering with Arden for All, an arts-education program that helps bring theatre experiences and teaching-artist instruction to students who might not otherwise have access.

Ari has performed with Haddonfield Theater Arts Company (HTAC) for years and loves acting — so for his mitzvah project, he wanted to share that joy and confidence-building experience with kids who have fewer opportunities.

He fundraised by distributing Candygrams around town for Valentine’s Day and raised over $1,000. He’s also volunteering in the classes with students in the city, helping them engage with performance and storytelling.

Thank you for supporting Ari’s project and the power of arts education.
